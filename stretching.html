Stretching

Stretching is important for everyone, not for only athletes. We all need to stretch in order to protect our mobility.

  1. Benefits of stretching
  2. Types of stretching
  3. Examples of exercises

Benefits of stretching

Why is it so important to stretch daily?

Stretching keeps the muscles flexible, strong and healthy, and we need that flexibility to maintain a range of motions int the joints.

Advantages of stretching:

  • Increases flexibility and range of motion
  • Improves posture and performance in physical activity
  • Increases blood flood to your muscles
  • Helps to heal and prevent back pain
  • Can reduce stress

Types of stretching

What kind of stretching should we do for the best result?

Different methods of stretching work for different people and goals. Maximal strength, number of repeptitions and total volume is different for each type.

The 3 most common stretching method

Method Description
Dynamic stretching It's a form of active stretching that is preformed by engaging the desired muscle's antagonist through the joint's range of motion, only holding the stretch for 2-3 seconds The muscle is able to increase in length without a reduction in muscle tension and therefore the individual is able to improve their range of motion without a loss in force production.
Static stretching This is the most commonly known style of stretching. It's a form of active or passive stretching in which you hold a position for about 30-60 seconds, allowing the muscles and their connective tissues to lengthen. But this type of stretching may not be the best way to improve performance before physical activity, because it may inhibit the muscle's ability to fire properly.
Ballistic stretching It utilizes muscle activation through quick, jerky movements. This inhibits the body's stretch reflex and increase the muscle's range of motion through the force created by the bouncing. Most fitness professionals do not recommend using this style, because the extra external force produced can overload the muscle, increasing the risk for potential injury.

Examples of stretching exercises

Dynamic stretching

  • Hip circles
  • Arm circles
  • Arm and leg swings
  • High stepping
  • Heel-to-toe walk

Static stretching

  • Hip stretch
  • Cobra pose
  • Child's pose
  • Cat cow stretch
  • Lower backstretch
  • Forward bend

Ballistic stretching

  • Standing toe stretch
  • Sitting toe stretch
  • Shoulder rotations
  • Swinging exercises for legs, arms and chest muscles
