Different types of workouts and their benefits

Benefits of working out

Why exercise is so important?

Exercising regularly has a lot of powerful health benefits, both physically and mentally. Being physically active is important, especially if you have a sedentary lifestyle, because it can raise your risk of gaining weight, heart disease, and other diseases. But exercising can help you prevent these and it's never too late to start!

Advantages of exercises

It can prevent health problems and diseases such as heart disease, many types of cancer, and high cholesterol. Exercise can improve the health of your brain and your memory, and help you manage your body weight. It can improve your mood, make you happier, increase your energy levels and self-esteem, strengthen bones and muscles, and overall improve your ability to do everyday activities.

Variety in your workout routine

Why is it important?

Having different types of workouts in your routine is important because each type of exercise has its benefits. Doing different types of workouts reduces the risk of overuse injuries, prevents boredom and fitness and weight loss plateaus, so your body can never get too comfortable with your workouts.

The 4 different types of workout that I will show you

  1. Endurance training
  2. Strength training
  3. Flexibility training
  4. Balance training

4 different types of workout

Endurance training

Endurance exercise, also called cardio exercise, increases your heart and breathing rate. It improves the health of your lungs, heart, and circulatory system. It can help prevent diseases such as heart disease and even certain cancers. Cardio exercise can help you lose weight or maintain your body weight. Examples of aerobic exercises:

  • running
  • walking
  • cycling
  • swimming
  • hiking
  • jumping rope

Strength training

Strength training is also called weight training, resistance training, and muscular training. It's the most popular type of training, especially in the gym. These types of exercises are movements designed to build muscle. Resistance training makes you stronger, burns calories, boosts your metabolism and mood, and improves the health of your heart and brain. It also decreases your risk of falls and injury, as you're better able to support your body. Here are some examples of exercises:

  • weightlifting
  • resistance band workouts
  • push-ups variations
  • squat variations
  • bicep curls

Flexibility training

Flexibility is incredibly important too, it's essential for avoiding injuries, particularly during endurance and strength training. The easiest way to improve your flexibility is by stretching. Stretching loosens up our muscles by elongating them, this is why it is important to stretch before and after a workout. Flexibility workouts help you improve your posture, prevent joint and muscle injuries prevent long-term pains and also lower your stress. Some examples of exercises:

  • stretching
  • yoga
  • pilates

Balance training

Balance is very important for all bodies, not just for athletes. Balance workouts help you increase stability, improve posture and agility, and prevent risk of falls and injuries. Examples of balance exercises:

  • single-leg exercises
  • yoga
  • pilates
