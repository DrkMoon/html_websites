Strength training

It's a method of training that helps you build muscle mass and increase your muscular strength. It makes you stronger and allows you perform daily tasks much easier.

Benefits of strength training

Why you should start doing strength exercises?

Building muscle can make your daily life easier. Strength training can help you feel more confident and capable of daily tasks like carrying groceries, lifting heavier objects, go upstairs.

Strength exercise benefits:

  • Improves muscle growth and tone
  • Stimulates bone growth and strength
  • Improves heart and brain health
  • Helps manage your blood sugar levels
  • Lowers your risk of falls and therefore risk of injuries
  • Boosts your mood and self-esteem

Equipment types

There are different types of equipments that you can use when you're training strength.

Different type of strength training may include

Equipment Description
Body weight Can be used for squats, push-ups and chin-ups. Convenient, especially when travelling or at work.
Resistance band Provides resistence when stretched. It's portable and can be adapted to most workouts. The band provides continuous resistance throughout a movement.
Free weights most used strength training tools such as dumbells, barbells and kettlebells.
Medicine balls or sand bags Weighted balls or bags.
Weight machines This devices have adjustable seats with handels attached either to weights os hydraulics.
Suspension equipment This tool uses gravity and the users body weight to complete exercises.

Examples of exercises

Group your muscles

Pairing certain muscles groups together in your training routine can help make your wourkouts more efficient. You can also use combo exercises in your routine, if you have little time for training.

Best exercises for beginners

  • Squat

    It targets your glutes, quads, and core muscles. It's better if you work on your form without weights and after your form is solid you can add weights. There are many variations of this exercise: single leg squat, sumo squat, barbell front and back squat, dumbell squat, split squat. You can read more about squats here: 7 different types of squat

  • Deadlift

    It targets your glutes and hamstrings. Because you're working from a stable base, you can really use some weights. There are a bunch of variatons such as sumo deadlift, romanian deadlift, single leg deadlift (can be done without weight).

  • Glute bridge

    Is a hip-extension exercise that targets the glutes, but also engages your hamstrings and the core muscles. You can do this exercise with weights or you can use a resistance band too.

  • Push up

    It builds your upper body muscles: deltoids of your shoulder, the pectoral muscles of the chest, the biceps and triceps of the upper arm. It also works your core, back and legs. There are many variations and each one targets a specific muscle.

  • Bent-over row

    It's a pulling exercise that uses all of the pulling muscles in your upper body, including biceps, shoulders and back. To maintain a strong position, you need to engage your core and legs, so it works these muscles too.

  • Plank

    It's an excellent abdominal and core exercise that makes your core strong and stable, and a strong core can reduce stress on the joints and improve your posture. Different types of plank exercises: reverse plank, side plank, straight-arm plank, hip dip plank.
